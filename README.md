# Optimizing E-Commerce Marketing Strategy: Gold Mines vs. Money Pits

## Problem Statement
Marketing teams often struggle to distinguish between channels and regions that drive sustainable growth versus those that drain budgets. The challenge was to analyze e‑commerce sales and marketing data to identify where each dollar spent creates the most value.

## Analytical Approach
### Defined the Objective:
- Measure efficiency of marketing spend using the LTV-to-CAC ratio.
- Frame analysis around identifying “Gold Mines” (high ROI) vs. “Money Pits” (low ROI).
- Data Preparation
- Collected performance data across products, regions, and competition tiers.
- Cleaned and structured datasets for comparability across segments.
  
### Dataset:
- Sales performance across products, regions, and competition tiers.
- Key metric: Lifetime Value (LTV) to Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) ratio.
- This dataset has been downloaded from (https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/amineipad/e-commerce-marketing-and-sales-revenue-prediction) and has been 'collected through an automated internal data pipeline that integrated the company's Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system', as mentioned by the author, hence is an authentic dataset. The dataset has been issued under the license (https://www.mit.edu/~amini/LICENSE.md) and copyright goes to rightful owners mentioned in the license.

### Exploratory Analysis:
- Competition Analysis: Compared CPA across low vs. high competition markets.
- Regional Performance: Evaluated revenue potential by geography.
- Product Profitability: Segmented categories by revenue vs. profitability.
- Channel Audit: Assessed acquisition channels by CLV and CPA.
  
### Key Findings:
- High‑competition markets are 1.3× more costly to enter.
- The North region consistently delivers the highest revenue potential.
- Storage drives revenue volume, while General category leads profitability.
- Search, Social Media, TV channels show the strongest CLV-to-CPA ratios.

### Strategic Recommendations:
- Reallocate spend toward Search, Social Media, and TV.
- Expand aggressively in the North region.
- Spend on Search, Social Media and TV while using Email selectively for high‑value segments.
- Shift from acquisition-heavy spend to retention & conversion optimization.
- Upscale in low-competition markets while acquisition costs remain low.

## Outcome
The analysis provided a data-driven roadmap for marketing optimization:
- Clear identification of high-value opportunities.
- Actionable strategies to reduce wasted spend.
- Balanced focus on short-term revenue growth and long-term profitability.
